Game Rules

Start the game by assigning people to one of three roles: clue-giver, checker, and guesser. The owner of the game starts off as the clue-giver (isn't capitalism great?), the person whose half-birthday is the nearest begins as the checker, and all other participants are guessers for the first round.

Each round, the clue-giver has sixty (60) seconds to clear as many cards as possible by using words, phrases, gestures, and even complete sentences to try to get the guessers to guess the secret word atop each card. In the process, nothing is off limits except for rhyming and the five (5) "out of bounds" words below, which the checker ensures the clue-giver does not use, else face a one (1) point penalty. The clue-giver may also skip a word he or she finds difficult for the same 1 point penalty.

Points can be earned only by the clue-giver in any given round, one for every card cleared. The guesser who correctly guesses the most secret words becomes the next clue-giver following each round; meanwhile, the clue-giver becomes the next checker and the checker returns to guessing duty.

The game can be played for as long or as short as you like, and there is no real limit to the number of players who can play together. Whenever you decide to stop, that player who has accrued the most points is the winner.
